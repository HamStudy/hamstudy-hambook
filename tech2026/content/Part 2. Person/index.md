# Part 2: The Person Behind Amateur Radio

Part 1 was about how radio works. Part 2 is about how to *operate* — how to set up a station, use it safely, follow the rules, and actually talk to other people. Knowing the physics gets you a radio that functions. Knowing the practices gets you a conversation, an event, a net, or a hand during an emergency.<span class="clear-footer"></span>

None of the material in Part 2 is as abstract as Part 1 — most of it is practical, and some of it is genuinely fun. But there's also material here that matters more than any formula: how to keep yourself from getting hurt, how to keep your signal from causing problems for other people, and how to stay within the rules of the service.

Part 2 has four chapters:

- **Safety** — electrical hazards, RF exposure, batteries, and how to install antennas without dying from it.
- **Station Setup** — assembling a working station, connecting it to the world, and troubleshooting when things go sideways.
- **Operating Practices** — what to say on the air (and what not to), how repeaters and nets work, and the operating conventions that keep things civil and legible.
- **Rules and Regulations** — the parts of FCC Part 97 that Technicians actually need to know, including frequency privileges, station identification, and what's permitted.

By the end, you should be ready to pass the exam — and more importantly, ready to get on the air.
