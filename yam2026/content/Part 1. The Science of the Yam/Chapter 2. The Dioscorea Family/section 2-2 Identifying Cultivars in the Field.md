Incorrect cultivar identification has real consequences: research records become unreliable, farmers select the wrong planting material, and cooks produce unexpected results. Field identification works by assembling converging physical evidence—no single trait is decisive on its own, but the pattern of multiple markers narrows identification reliably. ### Leaf and Vine Identification

Don't ignore the leaves! Most yams have simple, heart-shaped leaves, but the "Trifoliate yam" (*Dioscorea dumetorum*) is easily identified by its compound leaves with three leaflets—this species contains toxins requiring careful processing, making correct identification a safety decision, not just a botanical preference.

> **Key Information:** The Trifoliate yam (*Dioscorea dumetorum*) is identified by its compound leaves with three leaflets. {{< link id="Y1C05" >}}
