Propagation method determines what farmers can produce at scale, what diseases persist across generations, and what options breeders have for introducing new varieties. The critical difference from most other crops is this: while seed propagation introduces genetic variation and enables breeding, yam cultivation relies mainly on vegetative propagation—making identical copies of existing plants. This is efficient and predictable, but it also means that problems like viruses and dormancy are propagated along with the plant, and new variety development requires deliberate intervention. Every sett is a photocopy: it reproduces the variety's strengths and its weaknesses with equal fidelity. This is the fundamental bargain of vegetative propagation—speed and predictability in exchange for genetic lock-in.

### Vegetative Propagation

The primary method of propagating cultivated yams is by using tuber pieces, also known as "setts." This method optimizes for practicality and speed: a known variety can be reproduced at scale without waiting for seeds. However, it also propagates any pathogens present and locks the variety into its existing genetic package. 

> **Key Information:** The primary method of propagation for cultivated yams is vegetative propagation using tuber pieces (setts). {{< link id="Y7B01" >}}

Each sett must contain at least one viable bud to sprout and grow into a complete plant. The presence of these buds and proper moisture conditions are the most important factors for the sprouting of yam setts.

> **Key Information:** Proper moisture conditions and the presence of viable buds are critical for the sprouting of yam setts. {{< link id="Y7B08" >}} Activation of dormant buds is necessary for the tuber piece to regenerate into a new plant. {{< link id="Y7B09" >}}

Special techniques like the "minisett" system involve cutting tubers into very small pieces that each have at least one bud, allowing farmers to multiply their planting material more efficiently.

> **Key Information:** The minisett system involves cutting tubers into small pieces with at least one bud to increase planting material efficiency. {{< link id="Y7B03" >}}

### Propagation by Bulbils and Tissue Culture

Not all yams are propagated from tubers. *Dioscorea bulbifera*, the "air potato," is unique because it can also be propagated from its aerial bulbils.

> **Key Information:** *Dioscorea bulbifera* (air potato) can be propagated from aerial bulbils. {{< link id="Y7B07" >}}

In modern agriculture, tissue culture is increasingly used to produce disease-free planting material, ensuring that new crops are not infected by the many viruses that can plague yams.

> **Key Information:** Tissue culture is used to produce disease-free planting material for yams. {{< link id="Y7B04" >}}

### The Challenge of Seeds and Dormancy

While yams *can* reproduce sexually through seeds, this is less common in cultivation. Many cultivated varieties rarely flower or produce viable seeds.

> **Key Information:** Sexual reproduction (from seeds) is less common because many cultivated varieties rarely flower or produce viable seeds. {{< link id="Y7B02" >}}

Even when planting material is ready, it may not sprout immediately. Yams have a natural period of dormancy that must be overcome before they will grow. This period varies by species and must be completed for the tuber to sprout. A sett planted before dormancy ends looks identical to ready planting material but produces no growth—a silent failure that wastes an entire season. The danger is that dormancy is not visible: there is no reliable external indicator of whether a sett has completed its rest period. This makes proper curing and storage management the only practical control point—and the difference between a full field and an empty one.

> **Key Information:** Completion of a rest period (dormancy), which varies by species, is necessary for yam tubers to sprout. {{< link id="Y7B05" >}} Proper curing and storage under appropriate temperature and humidity are used to break this dormancy and promote sprouting. {{< link id="Y7B06" >}}
