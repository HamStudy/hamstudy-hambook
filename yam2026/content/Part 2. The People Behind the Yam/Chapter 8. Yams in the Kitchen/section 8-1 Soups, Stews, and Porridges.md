Because yams are high in starch, they're the perfect base for liquid-heavy dishes like soups, stews, and porridges. They act as a natural thickener and a hearty filler. The starch is the reason—and different preparation methods control how much thickening power is released.

### Fufu: The Heart of West Africa

Why would anyone spend twenty minutes pounding boiled yams by hand? Because the pounding does something no blender can replicate—it develops a stretchy, elastic texture by aligning starch molecules under physical force.

The most iconic West African yam dish is fufu.

> **Key Information:** The traditional pounding technique for fufu uses a wooden mortar and pestle to transform boiled yams into a smooth, stretchy paste. {{< link id="Y5E04" >}}

> **Key Information:** Fufu is a pounded yam paste eaten with stews and soups. {{< link id="Y5A04" >}}

### Porridges and Soups

A porridge stretches one yam into multiple meals—adding water and fat turns a single tuber into a dish that feeds a family. These are not recipes; they are caloric efficiency under scarcity.

Yams are also the main ingredient in several savory porridges.

> **Key Information:** Asaro is a Nigerian yam porridge cooked with palm oil and peppers. {{< link id="Y5B01" >}}

> **Key Information:** Mpotompoto is a Ghanaian dish that uses yam as the main ingredient in a savory porridge or soup. {{< link id="Y5B06" >}} {{< link id="Y5D06" >}}

Another unique soup is Ikokore, which takes a different approach to preparation.

> **Key Information:** Ikokore is a West African dish where grated water yam is formed into balls and cooked in a soup. {{< link id="Y5D01" >}}

### Global Liquid Dishes

In the Caribbean, yams are often boiled in mixed stews.

> **Key Information:** "Oil down" is a Caribbean dish that combines yams with other starchy vegetables and meat in a boiled preparation. {{< link id="Y5B03" >}}

In Vietnam, yams are used in "canh khoai mỡ," a soup that features purple yam.

> **Key Information:** Vietnamese "canh khoai mỡ" is prepared by simmering purple yam in a soup with pork. {{< link id="Y5B10" >}}

### Starchy Staples

When yams are used in stews, they serve as a hearty filler.

> **Key Information:** Yams are typically incorporated into stews by cutting them into chunks and adding them to cook with the other ingredients. {{< link id="Y5D03" >}}

One common point of confusion is "poi." While it's a fermented, starchy paste similar to fufu, it's actually made from taro, not yam.

> **Key Information:** Poi is made from taro in Pacific Island cuisines by cooking and pounding it into a fermented paste. {{< link id="Y5A09" >}}
